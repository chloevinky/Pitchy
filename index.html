💗 Take care of your voice

🔊 Resonance & Vocal Weight

How bright and forward your voice sounds — often more important than pitch for how a voice is perceived.

What am I looking at? (resonance, brightness, F1 & F2 explained)

Resonance is the "size and shape" your voice sounds like it comes from. A larger vocal tract (lower, chestier) sounds darker and more masculine; a smaller, more forward space sounds brighter and more feminine. You change it by where you place the sound — not by straining for height.

Brightness below is a quick spectral measure (the "centre of gravity" of your sound). Higher = brighter. It works on any speech, no special vowel needed — good for a fast at-a-glance read.

F1 and F2 are formants — the two strongest resonant peaks of your vocal tract. They're the most accurate resonance measure, but they depend on the vowel you say, so compare the same sustained vowel (hold an "eee" or "ah") across recordings. As resonance feminises, F1 and F2 both tend to rise.

These are estimates from your audio, not clinical measurements — use them to spot trends, not as exact targets.

💡 Voice Training Tips

  • Prioritise resonance over raw pitch — bright, forward resonance reads more feminine than a high pitch alone
  • Practice gently and daily; never strain to reach a target
  • Focus on resonance placement - chest vs head voice
  • Work with a gender-affirming voice coach or SLP for personalized guidance
  • Record yourself regularly to track progress
  • Remember: pitch is just one aspect of gender expression
